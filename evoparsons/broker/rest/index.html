Informed Consent to Participate in Research

Information to Consider Before Taking Part in this Research Study
Pro # 00037825
Researchers at the University of South Florida (USF) study many topics. To do this, we need the help of people who agree to take part in a research study. This form tells you about this research study. We are asking you to take part in a research study that is called - Experimental Comparison of Hand-designed vs. Evolved Parsons Puzzles for Novice Programmers (Pro00037825). The person who is in charge of this research study is Alessio Gaspar. This person is called the Principal Investigator.

Purpose of the Study
The purpose of this study is to find out what students who are learning computer programming think of practice exercises generated by the software our team developed. The research will be carried out by having participants use the software we developed, then take a brief online survey to share their thoughts about various aspect of their experience with it.

Why are you being asked to take part?
We are asking you to take part in this research study because you are enrolled in a course that our practice software has been specifically developed to support.

Study Procedures
Whether or not you decide to take part in this study, you will be able to do the following;

  • Use freely the programming practice software that our team has developed
  • Take the survey to allow you to share your thoughts about your experience with the software
Both the software and survey will ask you to identify yourselves so that we can then send back to your instructor a list of the students who participated to both the survey and used the software.Please note that, our team will ensure of your privacy by
  • Only sending back to your instructor the list of student names (without any further information) that participated. This is so that you may be assigned extra credit if your instructor decide to do so.
  • Not publishing or disseminating any information identifying you personally.
We have currently two versions of the software; one using evolved Parsons puzzles, and one using hand-designed ones. While there is evidence in the research literature that Parsons puzzles help students strengthen their programming skills, we don’t know if one of the two versions is better than the other. Therefore, students will be assigned randomly to one or the other version. This will allow us to keep access to the software fair for everyone.

The research will be completed at the University of South Florida, in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering.
If you decide to not participate in the study, see next section for details.

Alternatives / Voluntary Participation / Withdrawal
You have the alternative to choose not to participate in this research study. At any point during the assignment, you can email the PI at alessio@usf.edu and simply mention that you do not want to take part in this study. You will still have access to the software, the survey, and our team will forward your name to your instructor as for every student who used the software and took the survey. However, we will not use any of your data in our research.
You should only take part in this study if you want to volunteer; you are free to participate in this research or withdraw at any time. There will be no penalty or loss of benefits you are entitled to receive if you stop taking part in this study. Your decision to participate or not to participate will not affect your student status, course grade, recommendations, or access to future courses or training opportunities.

Benefits and Risks
The type of practice problems made available by our software are called Parsons puzzles. There has been significant evidence in the research literature over the past decade that these puzzle have a positive impact on the acquisition of programming skills. We expect that using the software will be helpful to you.
This research is considered to be minimal risk.

Compensation
We will not pay you for the time you volunteer while being in this study. However, your instructor of record will receive the list of all students who used the software, and all students who took the online survey. He or she will let you know whether they intend to assign any participation credit.

Privacy and Confidentiality
We must keep your study records as confidential as possible. It is possible, although unlikely, that unauthorized individuals could gain access to your responses because you are responding online. Certain people may need to see your study records. By law, anyone who looks at your records must keep them completely confidential. The only people who will be allowed to see these records are: the Principal Investigator and members of the research team, the University of South Florida Institutional Review Board (IRB), government offices such as, The Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS).
It is possible, although unlikely, that unauthorized individuals could gain access to your responses. Confidentiality will be maintained to the degree permitted by the technology used. No guarantees can be made regarding the interception of data sent via the Internet. However, your participation in this online survey involves risks similar to a person’s everyday use of the Internet.

Contact Information
If you have any questions about your rights as a research participant, please contact the USF IRB at (813) 974-5638 or contact by email at RSCH-IRB@usf.edu. If you have questions regarding the research, please contact the Principal Investigator at alessio@usf.edu.

We may publish what we learn from this study. If we do, we will not let anyone know your name. We will not publish anything else that would let people know who you are. You can print a copy of this consent form for your records.
